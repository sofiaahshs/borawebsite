ECONOMIC STATUS



The economy of Aklan posted a growth of 3.4 percent in 2021, a rebound from a -20.7 percent decline in the previous year. In 2021, the Gross Domestic Product (GPDP) of Aklan is valued at Php 51.9 billion, still lower than the pre-pandemic GDP level of Php 63.9 billion in 2019. The province of Aklan is designated as a first-class province. Agriculture is the main occupation, with rice and corn produced in abundance. The topography favors the development of the fishpond industry and coconut plantations. Fishing is a major industry, and there is a National College of Fisheries in New Washington



Aside from its vibrant tourism industry, farming is the basic livelihood in the interior while fishing is the basic livelihood in the coasts. The province posts potentials in the production of high valued and fibre crops. Aklan depends greatly on agriculture.
